\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

A century after \citet{kitchin1923}, \citet{juglar1862},
\citet{kuznets1930} and \citet{kondratieff1925} introduced the
four canonical economic cycles, the empirical status of those
cycles remains contested. The standard reading in long-wave and
econophysics work
\citep{perez2002,modelski1987,korotayev_tsirel2010} is
universalist: a single canonical period at the heart of every
macroeconomic time series. The standard reading in the empirical
business-cycle literature is sceptical: \citet{burns_mitchell1946}
refused to commit to any canonical period;
\citet{garvy1943,solomou1987,romer1999,stock_watson2003,wen2005}
have argued in turn that no canonical periodicity is universal,
and that what looks like Kondratieff, Kuznets, Juglar or Kitchin
on composite aggregates often does not appear on the underlying
variables. The two readings have rarely been confronted on the
same data through the same protocol.

This paper provides that confrontation. We apply a single
falsifiable protocol---three gates and a per-variable
safeguard---to every cell of the
\(\{\text{cycle}\}\times\{\text{panel}\}\times\{\text{country / aggregate}\}\times\{\text{variable}\}\)
grid across five independent panels spanning 1700--2024, with
threshold values committed to a public Git history before the
panels were ingested.

\paragraph{Headline finding.}
The empirical answer that emerges is more nuanced than either
extreme reading admits. The canonical cycles are alive on exactly
the variables the substantive literature predicts:

\begin{itemize}
\item the \textbf{Juglar band} passes Gate~1 on
  \textbf{investment-to-GDP and unemployment} on the JST R6
  advanced economies (1.9-fold excess on 605 testable cells),
  and on the quarterly G7Q/OECDQ unemployment aggregates
  (4.3-fold excess on 55 cells);
\item the \textbf{Kuznets band} passes on the
  \textbf{house-price index in 8 of 13 testable JST countries
  (62\,\%)}, on population (39\,\%) and on credit aggregates
  (41\,\%, 2.2-fold excess on 529 cells);
\item the \textbf{Kitchin band} passes on the \textbf{BIS
  emerging-market credit aggregates} (credit-to-GDP gap and
  ratio, household and business credit, real property prices) on
  Korea, China, Mexico, South Africa, Turkey, Russia, Indonesia
  (5.3-fold excess on 93 cells), and on the BRICS / WLD / LMC
  trade and inflation composites on the WB annual panel;
\item the \textbf{Kondratieff band} passes on \textbf{UK public
  debt-to-GDP on BoE Millennium 1700--2016} (\(p_1=0.004\)),
  with the same variable also passing Kuznets ---the
  Reinhart-Rogoff debt-cycle reading vindicated.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{What we therefore claim, and what we do not.}
We claim that the substantive theory of each canonical cycle is
empirically alive: Juglar on investment-financed business cycles
\citep{schumpeter1939,juglar1862}, Kuznets on long swings in
demographics and construction \citep{kuznets1930}, Kitchin on
inventory and financial cycles \citep{kitchin1923,wen2005,borio2014},
Kondratieff on debt-cycle long waves driven by exogenous shocks
\citep{kondratieff1925,reinhart_rogoff2009}. We do \emph{not}
claim that any single canonical period applies universally to
every macroeconomic variable, and we do not claim that the joint
collection of unadjusted positives survives Benjamini-Hochberg
adjustment at FDR 0.05 on the full grid at our surrogate counts
(it does not). The negative result is properly scoped to the
strong universalist sinusoidal-on-everything reading; the
positive result vindicates the substantive theory on the named
channels.

\paragraph{The protocol.}
Three gates cascade. Gate~1 (existence) requires the empirical
band-power inside the canonical band to exceed an
\(\AR{1}\)-bootstrap null at \(\alpha=0.05\); a complementary
phase-coherence diagnostic
\citep{theiler_et_al1992,schreiber_schmitz2000} is reported
alongside but is not the load-bearing gate (Section~\ref{sec:methods}).
Gate~2 (consensus) requires agreement of at least three of four
heterogeneous estimators (Christiano-Fitzgerald, Morlet,
Hamilton-switching, PELT). Gate~3 (cross-aggregate concordance)
counts how many of the World Bank income-stratified aggregates
agree, without treating asymmetric outcomes as failure (this is
not a universality test by construction:
Section~\ref{sec:criteria}). A per-variable safeguard
\citep{wen2005} filters composite-aggregation artefacts.

\paragraph{Calibration.}
The empirical power of the protocol is well-mapped. On
AR(1)+cosine alternatives at \(\mathrm{SNR}\geq 0.3\), Gate~1
detects 100\,\% of cycles
(Appendix~\ref{app:methods}). On Markov-switching and
multifractal alternatives the power is lower (3--26\% across SNR)
and we discuss the implications: the protocol's verdict is
properly scoped to the linear-sinusoidal reading of each
canonical cycle, which is precisely what the original
historical claims made.

\paragraph{Commitment of thresholds.}
The cycle bands, surrogate counts, gate thresholds and
aggregate groupings were committed to the project's public Git
history before any panel was ingested. We do not claim this is
a formal pre-registration in the sense of OSF or the AEA RCT
Registry, and we make the distinction explicit in
Appendix~\ref{app:criteria}. The threshold values themselves
are anchored to the prior empirical literature, not derived
from the data analysed here.
