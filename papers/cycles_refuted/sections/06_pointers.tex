\section{Pointers toward complementary non-cyclical signatures}
\label{sec:pointers}

The variable-specific positive result of
Section~\ref{sec:results} establishes that the canonical
periodicities live where the substantive theory predicts. It
does \emph{not} preclude the existence of additional,
non-cyclical structural features of macroeconomic series. This
section sketches an empirical signature that, in companion
work, we find on the same panels: long memory, multifractality,
non-linearity, information complexity and reflexive drift. The
sketch is short because the development is the subject of a
separate methodological paper currently in preparation.

\paragraph{A five-family non-cyclical signature.}
On the same panels and at the same significance level, five
diagnostic families consistently reject the AR(1) null on a
majority of cells: long memory (Hurst exponent estimated by
detrended fluctuation analysis \citep{peng1994,hurst1951}),
multifractality (MF-DFA spectrum width
\citep{kantelhardt2002,bacry_muzy_delour2001}), non-linearity
(BDS independence test
\citep{brock_dechert_scheinkman1996}), information complexity
(permutation entropy \citep{bandt_pompe2002}) and reflexive
drift (sliding-window Kolmogorov-Smirnov tests on
regime-conditional distributions). These diagnostics are
\emph{complementary} to, not substitutes for, the canonical
cyclical reading of
Section~\ref{sec:results}: they describe scaling, memory and
heterogeneity structure that lives orthogonally to
periodicity.

\paragraph{Generative models.}
Multifractal random walks \citep{bacry_muzy_delour2001,
calvet_fisher2002,calvet_fisher2008} provide a generative
model that reproduces the empirical scaling signature.
Adaptive markets \citep{lo2017} and the free-energy principle
\citep{friston2010} provide a behavioural rationale for the
reflexive component. Random matrix theory
\citep{marchenko_pastur1967,laloux1999,bouchaud_potters2003}
characterises the cross-sectional structure.

\paragraph{Status of the companion paper.}
The companion paper is in preparation. We do not seek journal
submission of the present paper until the companion is
available as a citeable preprint, so that the variable-specific
canonical-cycle finding and the orthogonal non-cyclical
signature are on record together. The two papers are intended
to be read as a pair: this paper documents \emph{where} the
canonical cycles live and where the universalist reading
fails; the companion documents the non-cyclical statistical
features that hold across the same panels independently of any
period.
