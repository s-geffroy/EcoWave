\section{Data}
\label{sec:data}

A central concern of any cycle-falsification claim is data quality:
weak rejection of a cycle on a short or noisy series proves little.
We therefore assemble six heterogeneous panels, each independently
sourced and each long enough to host several full periods of the
longest cycle under test (Kondratieff, 40--60y). Cross-panel
agreement and cross-source consistency over overlapping windows
are explicit gates of the protocol (Section~\ref{sec:criteria}).
Appendix~\ref{app:data} lists every variable with source URL,
licence, access date and SHA-256 checksum, and documents
pre-processing decisions (deflation, log-difference, splicing,
gap-filling).

\paragraph{Coverage of the longest band.}
The Kondratieff band (40--60y) requires at least \(\approx 2.5\) full
periods for a Gate~1 dual-null test to have meaningful power
\citep{torrence_compo1998}. Only the Bank of England Millennium panel
(316y of annual UK data) and the Jord\`a-Schularick-Taylor R6 panel
(150y, eighteen advanced economies) provide that coverage. Both are
included precisely so that a critic cannot dismiss a Kondratieff
rejection as sample-size driven.

\paragraph{Frequency for the shortest band.}
The Kitchin band (3--5y) is borderline Nyquist on annual data: a
3-year period maps to a frequency of \(0.333\,\mathrm{y}^{-1}\), within
a factor 1.5 of the annual Nyquist limit. We therefore include a
quarterly panel (Q, 1995--2024) so that the Kitchin band can be tested
at \(4\times\) finer resolution.

\paragraph{Composites versus components.}
The empirical literature contains a recurring artefact whereby a
composite aggregate exhibits a cycle that none of its component
variables exhibits individually \citep{wen2005}. Our protocol enforces
a per-variable safeguard (Section~\ref{sec:criteria}) and the
sectoral panel exists precisely to test cycles on raw, unaggregated
historical series (rail freight, pig iron, cotton, wheat, etc.).

\subsection{Reproducibility}
\label{sec:repro}

Every raw payload is fingerprinted with SHA-256 and stored in the
project SQLite catalogue (\path{ecowave/db.py:raw_files}). The full
ingestion pipeline runs end-to-end in a Docker container in
\(\approx 30\) minutes and emits JSON sidecars conforming to a
versioned schema. Appendix~\ref{app:data} reports cross-source
consistency on overlapping windows (JST vs.\ BoE on UK 1870--2016;
BIS vs.\ JST on G7 credit-to-GDP 1970--2020): pass-rates are within
the pre-registered \(\pm 5\%\) tolerance.

\subsection{Licences and ethics}

All sources are public-domain or licensed for academic
redistribution (World Bank CC~BY~4.0; FRED public-domain; Bank of
England OBRA citation-required; JST academic-citation; BIS
citation-required). No proprietary or personally identifying data
are used. Licence texts are listed in Appendix~\ref{app:data}.
