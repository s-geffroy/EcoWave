\section{Results}
\label{sec:results}

We apply the three-gate protocol to every cell of the
\(\{\text{Kitchin, Juglar, Kuznets, Kondratieff}\}\times\{\text{six
panels}\}\) grid; the income-stratified Gate~\ref{gate:3} is run on
the WB panel whose income aggregates are well-defined. The
per-variable safeguard (Section~\ref{sec:safeguard}) is enforced
throughout.

\subsection{Headline verdict}

\paragraph{No cycle is universal.}
Across the cascade, no canonical cycle simultaneously passes
Gates~\ref{gate:1}--\ref{gate:3} on more than two of the six panels.
Kitchin survives on the World Bank composite (WLD), driven by the
Asian inventory-cycle signature documented by \citet{wen2005}, but
collapses to component-level non-significance under the safeguard.
Juglar survives on the long-history panels (JST R6 and BoE) before
1945, consistent with \citet{romer1999} and \citet{stock_watson2003}:
the post-war Great Moderation has erased the inter-war Juglar
signature. Kuznets fails on all annual panels and reaches Gate~\ref{gate:2}
only on the quarterly USA series, driven by post-1960 residential
construction swings rather than a generic 15--25y cycle. Kondratieff
is the most fragile of the four: no individual variable on any
panel survives the per-variable safeguard.

\paragraph{Composite-aggregation artefacts are pervasive.}
Where the composite passes Gate~\ref{gate:1} but the component
variables do not, we record a compositing artefact. This pattern
accounts for the bulk of the apparent positive results in the
literature and is exactly the diagnosis of \citet{wen2005} on
inventory cycles. Section~\ref{sec:wen_replication} reproduces that
test on a sectoral panel and on JST R6 in detail.

\paragraph{Robustness across panels.}
The verdict is invariant to source: applying the cascade to the
overlapping window of BoE and JST R6 (1870--2016, UK) yields the
same A/B/C/D pattern within the pre-registered \(\pm 5\%\)
agreement tolerance.

\subsection{Verdict by cycle}

\paragraph{Kitchin (3--5y).}
The composite-level result on the WB panel is positive
(\(p_\text{dual}=0.002\) on world GDP, dominated by the post-2000
Asian inventory swing). Under the per-variable safeguard, the
positive result disappears: no individual non-financial component
of the WB composite survives Gate~\ref{gate:1}. On the sectoral
panel (eleven historical series), zero of forty-four cells survive.
The verdict reproduces \citet{wen2005}: an inventory cycle exists
on dedicated inventory variables but is invisible on macro
composites.

\paragraph{Juglar (7--11y).}
On the post-1960 WB panel, the Juglar band fails Gate~\ref{gate:1}
on all but one income aggregate (\(p_\text{dual}>0.2\)). On the
long-history panels (BoE 1700+, JST R6 1870--2020), the Juglar band
passes Gates~\ref{gate:1}--\ref{gate:2} on G7 and ANGLO subsamples
but collapses when restricted to post-1945, reproducing the
\citet{romer1999} Great Moderation result.

\paragraph{Kuznets (15--25y).}
Universally rejected on all annual panels at Gate~\ref{gate:1}
(\(p_\text{dual}>0.2\)) except the WLD composite which is
borderline (\(p\approx0.05\)). The quarterly USA and GBR series pass
Gate~\ref{gate:2} on residential construction variables, consistent
with \citet{klotz_neal1973}'s and \citet{solomou1987}'s view that
Kuznets ``long swings'' are at most a regional construction-cycle
phenomenon, not a universal macroeconomic cycle.

\paragraph{Kondratieff (40--60y).}
The composite-level test on the WB panel shows world GDP, HIC and
OECD aggregates with \(p_\text{dual}=0.001\), apparently passing
Gate~\ref{gate:1}. The per-variable safeguard, however, eliminates
the result: zero individual variables on JST R6 (45 variables \(\times\)
18 economies = 810 cells) survive Gate~\ref{gate:1} on the Kondratieff
band, and zero individual UK variables on BoE 1700--2016 do either.
The composite positives are entirely driven by aggregation noise of
the post-1945 rising trend, which a 60-year window cannot
distinguish from a Kondratieff upswing. This conclusion is consistent
with \citet{garvy1943}, \citet{solomou1987} and \citet{maddison1991}.

\subsection{Per-variable replication of \citet{wen2005}}
\label{sec:wen_replication}

To test composite-aggregation artefacts head-on we reproduce
\citet{wen2005}'s setup on the sectoral panel: eleven historical
series (rail freight, pig iron, coal, cotton, wheat, etc.), four
cycle bands, dual-null Gate~\ref{gate:1} with \(B=1000\) surrogates.
The result, summarised in Appendix~\ref{app:per_variable}, is that
\emph{zero of forty-four} cells survive Gate~\ref{gate:1}---a 0\%
survival rate. The only positive results in the literature on these
sectoral series \citep{wen2005}'s inventory-cycle finding excepted)
arise from time-aggregation or composite formation. The safeguard
detects them and rejects them.

\subsection{What survives}

The cascade is not vacuous: it accepts the existence of an
inventory-style 3--5y cycle on dedicated inventory variables
(consistent with the original RBC literature
\citep{wen2005}) and the existence of a regional residential
construction cycle on post-1960 US and UK quarterly data
(consistent with \citet{klotz_neal1973}). No canonical cycle of
the four-cycle taxonomy survives as a \emph{universal} macroeconomic
phenomenon.
