\section{Acceptance criteria: three gates and a safeguard}
\label{sec:criteria}

A protocol that rejects no cycle anywhere is uninformative; a protocol
that accepts every cycle somewhere is unfalsifiable. We commit, before
data ingestion, to a cascade of three gates whose conjunction must be
satisfied to declare a cycle present. The full pre-registration appears
in Appendix~\ref{app:criteria} and is also written to the project
documentation file
\path{methodology/trois_portes.md}, with version-controlled history.

\begin{gate}[Existence]
\label{gate:1}
At significance level \(\alpha=0.05\) with \(B=1000\) surrogates, the
empirical band-power inside the canonical cycle band must dominate
\emph{both} the AR(1)-bootstrap null and the Theiler phase-randomised
null (Section~\ref{sec:dual_null}). Failure on \emph{either} null is
a rejection.
\end{gate}

\begin{gate}[Method consensus]
\label{gate:2}
At least three of the four estimators of Section~\ref{sec:methods}
(Christiano-Fitzgerald + Hilbert, Morlet band-power, Markov-switching
AR(1) and PELT) must agree on the dominant phase at every time-point
within the panel's window. The consensus threshold is relaxed to two
votes for Kitchin on annual data (where PELT and Bry-Boschan are
under-powered by sampling) and for Kondratieff on shorter panels
(where regime-switching cannot deliver enough state changes); these
relaxations are themselves pre-registered.
\end{gate}

\begin{gate}[Universality]
\label{gate:3}
Within an income-stratified family of aggregates (WLD, HIC, UMC, LMC,
LIC---the World Bank groupings), at least four of the five aggregates
must independently pass Gates~\ref{gate:1}--\ref{gate:2}. A cycle that
survives on a single aggregate is reported as \emph{regional}, not
\emph{universal}.
\end{gate}

The three gates form a cascade: a cell that fails at Gate~\(k\) is
not tested at Gate~\(k+1\). Verdicts are reported on a four-level
scale (A: universal, B: regional, C: disputed, D: rejected).

\subsection{Per-variable safeguard against compositing artefacts}
\label{sec:safeguard}

A well-known failure mode of cycle tests \citep{wen2005} is that
composite aggregates (\emph{e.g.}, world GDP) can exhibit
periodicities that none of their component variables exhibit
individually, because the aggregation smooths idiosyncratic noise
into apparent coherence. The protocol therefore mandates a
per-variable replication of Gate~\ref{gate:1} on every input variable
\emph{before} composite results are accepted. A composite-positive,
component-negative result is recorded as a compositing artefact and
does not contribute to the verdict.

This per-variable safeguard reproduces Wen's (2005) test on a
sectoral panel of eleven historical series (rail freight, pig iron,
coal, cotton, wheat, etc.). It is the most conservative safeguard
we are aware of in the literature.

\subsection{Choice of \(\alpha\) and multiplicity}

We fix \(\alpha=0.05\). We do \emph{not} apply a Bonferroni or
Benjamini-Hochberg correction across the (cycle, panel, variable) grid.
The cascade structure provides multiplicity protection by construction:
the joint type-I error of Gate~\(1\cap\)Gate~\(2\cap\)Gate~\(3\) is
bounded above by \(\alpha\) on any single cell (dual null) times the
probability of four-of-five panel agreement under the null, which is
much smaller than \(\alpha\). A formal derivation appears in
Appendix~\ref{app:criteria}; the upshot is that the cascade is
\emph{more} conservative than a Bonferroni-corrected single-gate
test.

\subsection{What would falsify the protocol itself?}

The protocol is itself a conjecture. We declare it falsified if any
of the following holds: (i) on a synthetic AR(1) panel, the false
positive rate at Gate~\ref{gate:1} exceeds \(0.05\) by more than
two standard errors over \(N=1000\) replicates; (ii) on a synthetic
panel with an embedded cosine at the canonical period, the power
to detect at Gate~\ref{gate:1} is below \(0.8\) at signal-to-noise
ratio 1.0; (iii) any of the four estimators fails to reproduce its
canonical published result on a textbook benchmark
(\emph{e.g.}, the Bry-Boschan estimator on the original NBER
business-cycle dating). All three conditions are routinely checked
in the project's test-suite (\path{ecowave/tests/}); the calibration
results are reported in Appendix~\ref{app:criteria}.
