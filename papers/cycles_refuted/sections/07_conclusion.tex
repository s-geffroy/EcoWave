\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We applied a uniform falsifiable protocol to the four canonical
economic cycles---Kitchin, Juglar, Kuznets, Kondratieff---across
five independent macroeconomic panels spanning 1700--2024. The
finding has two halves.

\paragraph{Positive: the canonical cycles are alive where theory predicts.}
Each of the four canonical cycles passes Gate~1 at the
unadjusted significance level on exactly the variables the
substantive economic literature predicts. Juglar is alive on
investment-to-GDP (39\,\% of testable JST advanced economies)
and on unemployment (the quarterly panel exhibits a clean
G7Q / OECDQ / GBR / JPN unemployment signature). Kuznets is
alive on house prices (46\,\% of testable JST countries) and on
population and credit (39--41\,\%). Kitchin is alive on the
BIS emerging-market credit aggregates on the quarterly panel
(5.3-fold excess on 93 cells) and on emerging-market trade
and financial flows on the WB annual panel. Kondratieff is
alive on UK public debt / GDP on the BoE Millennium panel,
consistent with the Reinhart-Rogoff debt-cycle reading. The
positive concentrations are not statistical noise: they cluster
on the variables \citet{kitchin1923}, \citet{juglar1862},
\citet{kuznets1930}, \citet{schumpeter1939} and
\citet{borio2014} each independently named. The substantive
theory of each cycle is empirically vindicated on the channels
each was originally claimed to govern.

\paragraph{Negative: the strong universalist reading fails.}
The joint collection of unadjusted positives does not survive
Benjamini-Hochberg adjustment at FDR 0.05 on the full
1\,456-cell grid at our surrogate counts. The strong
universalist sinusoidal-on-everything reading---a single
canonical period at the heart of every macroeconomic variable
on every panel---is rejected. This rejection reproduces, on a
uniform pre-registered protocol, the empirical scepticism that
has been building since
\citet{garvy1943,solomou1987,romer1999,stock_watson2003,wen2005}.

\paragraph{Methodological lessons.}
Three lessons follow. First, the canonical cycles should be
read as variable-specific phenomena, not as universal
periodicities. A Juglar cycle on aggregate GDP and a Juglar
cycle on investment are two empirically different claims; only
the latter is supported by our protocol. Second, the
composite-versus-component distinction \citep{wen2005} matters:
many of the unadjusted positives on emerging-market BIS panels
arise on individual credit variables, not on aggregate output
composites. Third, the empirical power of any cycle-detection
protocol against realistic non-linear alternatives must be
reported explicitly; our protocol is highly powerful against
linear sinusoidal cycles
(\(\geq 80\%\) at \(\mathrm{SNR}\geq 0.2\) on AR(1)+cosine)
but weak against Markov-switching and multifractal
alternatives.

\paragraph{Implications for empirical practice.}
A practitioner who wishes to model the Juglar cycle should
build on investment-to-GDP and unemployment, not on real GDP
composites. A practitioner modelling the Kuznets swing should
build on house prices, population and mortgage credit. A
practitioner modelling the Kitchin cycle on emerging markets
should build on the BIS credit aggregates. These are precisely
the channels the substantive macroeconomic literature has been
recommending; our contribution is to put the empirical map on
a uniform falsifiable protocol that can be reproduced and
revised.

\paragraph{Status of the companion paper.}
The present paper is intended to be read together with a
companion paper documenting non-cyclical statistical features
of the same panels (long memory, multifractality, non-linearity,
information complexity, reflexive drift); the companion paper is
in preparation and the present paper will not be submitted for
journal review until the companion is available as a citeable
preprint.

\section*{Reproducibility and open science}

The full pipeline---data ingestion, gate evaluation,
per-variable safeguard, calibration on synthetic alternatives,
generation of figures and tables---runs end-to-end in a Docker
container shipped with this paper. Every raw payload is
fingerprinted with SHA-256 and stored in a versioned SQLite
catalogue. Threshold commitments are recorded as dated Git tags.
The container, data manifests, validation scripts (DOI/ISBN
resolution against the CrossRef API; bibliographic consistency
check \(\{\backslash\)cite\(\}\leftrightarrow\) \texttt{.bib})
and LaTeX sources are deposited as part of the project
repository and will be archived to Zenodo with a permanent DOI
upon journal acceptance. Every numerical result in
Section~\ref{sec:results} and Appendix~\ref{app:per_variable}
comes from a reproducible empirical run; the scripts that
produce them are \path{scripts/calibrate_dual_null.py},
\path{scripts/wen_replication.py},
\path{scripts/boe_kondratieff_per_variable.py},
\path{scripts/jst_per_variable.py},
\path{scripts/wb_per_variable.py} and
\path{scripts/quarterly_per_variable.py}; the outputs they
write are the corresponding \texttt{*\_per\_variable.json}
sidecars under \path{reports/}.
