\section{Pre-registered acceptance criteria}
\label{app:criteria}

This appendix records, verbatim, the three-gate acceptance protocol
that was committed to the project repository before any data
ingestion. The protocol is also versioned in
\path{methodology/trois_portes.md} and frozen in code through the
immutable \texttt{MappingProxyType} export of
\path{ecowave/cycles/bands.py:CYCLE_BANDS}.

\subsection{Falsifiability statement}

A cycle is declared \emph{present} on a (cycle, panel, variable)
cell iff the cell passes Gates~\ref{gate:1}, \ref{gate:2} and
\ref{gate:3}. A cycle is declared \emph{absent} on a cell iff any
of the three gates is failed. The verdict is reported on a
four-level scale: A (universal), B (regional), C (disputed),
D (rejected).

\subsection{Gate~1: dual-null existence}

At significance level \(\alpha=0.05\) on \(B=1000\) surrogates per
null, the empirical band-power must exceed the 95th percentile of
\emph{both} (a) the AR(1)-bootstrap null and (b) the Theiler
phase-randomised null. Formally, with \(P_0\) the empirical
band-power and \(\{P^{(1)}_b\}_{b=1}^B,\{P^{(2)}_b\}_{b=1}^B\) the
two surrogate samples,
\begin{equation}
p_\mathrm{dual} \;=\; \max\!\left(
  \frac{1+\#\{b: P^{(1)}_b\geq P_0\}}{1+B},\;
  \frac{1+\#\{b: P^{(2)}_b\geq P_0\}}{1+B}
\right) < 0.05.
\end{equation}
Note the conservative \((B+1)\) denominator and \(+1\) numerator
recommended by \citet{schreiber_schmitz2000}.

\subsection{Gate~2: method consensus}

A cell passes Gate~\ref{gate:2} iff at least three of the four
estimators (Christiano-Fitzgerald, Morlet, Markov-switching,
PELT) cast the same phase label (or the matching dwell-band) at every
common time point. Pre-registered relaxations: two-of-two for
Kitchin on annual data and two-of-three for Kondratieff on the
World Bank panel (whose 65-year window admits one full PELT
changepoint at most).

\subsection{Gate~3: cross-aggregate universality}

A cycle passes Gate~\ref{gate:3} iff at least four of the five
income aggregates of the World Bank panel (WLD, HIC, UMC, LMC,
LIC) independently pass Gates~\ref{gate:1}--\ref{gate:2}. A cycle
that passes on fewer than four aggregates is recorded as
\emph{regional}; a cycle that passes on three or fewer is recorded
as \emph{rejected at universality}.

\subsection{Multiplicity}

We do \emph{not} apply Bonferroni or Benjamini-Hochberg corrections
across the (cycle, panel, variable) grid. The cascade structure
provides multiplicity protection by construction: under the joint
null (no cycle), the probability of passing all three gates on a
single cell is bounded above by
\(\alpha\cdot p_2\cdot p_3\), where
\(p_2\leq\binom{4}{3}\alpha^2\) (three-of-four consensus under
independent estimator nulls) and
\(p_3\leq\binom{5}{4}\alpha^4\) (four-of-five aggregate agreement
under panel independence). The order-of-magnitude joint type-I
error per cell is thus \(\sim\alpha^7\), making a Bonferroni
correction unnecessary.

\subsection{Per-variable safeguard}

A composite-positive, component-negative outcome is recorded as
a compositing artefact and does not contribute to the verdict.
Formally, a cycle passes the safeguard iff
\begin{equation}
\frac{\#\{v\in\mathcal{V}_\mathrm{composite}: p_\mathrm{dual}^{(v)}<0.05\}}
     {|\mathcal{V}_\mathrm{composite}|} \;\geq\; 0.5,
\end{equation}
where \(\mathcal{V}_\mathrm{composite}\) is the set of component
variables that enter the composite aggregate. The threshold
\(0.5\) reproduces the convention of \citet{wen2005}: a cycle
that is present on the composite but absent on more than half its
components is rejected as a compositing artefact.

\subsection{Conditions that would falsify the protocol itself}

The protocol is a conjecture. We declare it falsified on observation
of any of the following:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] False-positive rate at Gate~\ref{gate:1} on synthetic
  AR(1) panels exceeds \(\alpha=0.05\) by more than two standard
  errors over \(N=1000\) replicates.
\item[(ii)] Power to detect at Gate~\ref{gate:1} on synthetic panels
  with an embedded cosine at the canonical period (signal-to-noise
  ratio 1.0) falls below \(0.8\).
\item[(iii)] Any of the four Gate~\ref{gate:2} estimators fails to
  reproduce its canonical published result on the corresponding
  textbook benchmark (Bry-Boschan: NBER business-cycle dating;
  Hamilton: US industrial production 1947--1995;
  PELT: AR-coal-output benchmark of \citet{killick_fearnhead_eckley2012};
  Christiano-Fitzgerald: monthly US industrial production
  \citep{christiano_fitzgerald2003}).
\end{enumerate}
All three conditions are routinely checked by the project's
test-suite (\path{tests/}); calibration results are exported to
\path{reports/calibration_v1.json}.
