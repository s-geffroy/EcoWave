\section{Per-variable evidence, sensitivity, and FDR-adjusted verdict}
\label{app:per_variable}

This appendix reports the per-variable Gate~\ref{gate:1} evidence
that underpins Section~\ref{sec:results}, with raw \(p\)-values
rather than pass/fail indicators, with sensitivity to the
safeguard threshold and to the analysis window, and with the
Benjamini-Hochberg false-discovery-rate adjusted verdict as a
robustness check.

\subsection{Sensitivity to the analysis window}

We re-run Gate~\ref{gate:1} on each panel under two robustness
windows. The qualitative verdict (zero cells positive) is
invariant for the sectoral panel. For the JST R6 Kondratieff
result discussed in Section~\ref{sec:results}, the window
sensitivity is the key fact: 14 cells of 810 are positive on
1870--2020, but only 5 of those 14 remain positive when
restricted to 1870--2007. The dependency on the post-2008
window is consistent with the interpretation that the positive
results reflect post-crisis trend behaviour rather than an
underlying 40--60y oscillation.

\subsection{Benjamini-Hochberg FDR-adjusted verdict}

As a robustness check we apply Benjamini-Hochberg correction
\citep{benjamini_hochberg1995} at FDR 0.05 to the full grid of
\(1\,238\) Gate~\ref{gate:1} \(p_1\) values across all panels
and cycles. Under FDR-correction:

\begin{itemize}
\item No Kondratieff cell on any panel remains positive (the 14
  unadjusted positive cells on JST R6 all drop below the
  FDR-adjusted threshold).
\item Three Kitchin cells remain positive: the WB world GDP
  composite (an aggregation artefact under the per-variable
  safeguard), and two USA quarterly inventory-proxy variables.
\item No Juglar cell on annual post-1960 data remains positive;
  the long-history Juglar passages on G7 pre-1945 do remain.
\item One Kuznets cell remains positive: USA quarterly real
  residential property prices.
\end{itemize}

The FDR-adjusted picture is qualitatively the same as the
unadjusted cascade verdict, with one important difference: the
small set of FDR-positive cells corresponds exactly to the
known regional or sectoral exceptions (inventory cycles on
US quarterly data; residential construction on USA housing),
which the substantive literature has already documented. The
canonical universalist claim is rejected in both perspectives.

\subsection{Sensitivity to the safeguard threshold}

For thresholds in \(\{0.3, 0.5, 0.7\}\), the qualitative
verdict on every panel is invariant. The only cell whose
status depends on the threshold is the WB world Kitchin composite
(rejected at any threshold \(\geq 0.3\) under the safeguard;
admitted only if no safeguard is applied). We retain
\(0.5\) as the primary value following \citet{wen2005}'s usage.
