# Bibliographie

> **Résumé.** Liste consolidée de toutes les références citées sur le site
> (méthodologie, cycles, analyses). Style auteur-année. Les ancres `#auteur-annee`
> sont stables et utilisées par les liens internes des autres pages.

## A

### Aguiar-Conraria & Soares (2014) {#aguiar-conraria-soares-2014}

Aguiar-Conraria, L., & Soares, M. J. (2014). The continuous wavelet transform:
moving beyond uni- and bivariate analysis. *Journal of Economic Surveys*,
28(2), 344–375.

## B

### Borio & Drehmann (2009) {#borio-drehmann-2009}

Borio, C., & Drehmann, M. (2009). Assessing the risk of banking crises —
revisited. *BIS Quarterly Review*, March 2009, 29–46.

### Bry & Boschan (1971) {#bry-boschan-1971}

Bry, G., & Boschan, C. (1971). *Cyclical analysis of time series: selected
procedures and computer programs*. NBER Technical Paper 20.

## C

### Christiano & Fitzgerald (2003) {#christiano-fitzgerald-2003}

Christiano, L. J., & Fitzgerald, T. J. (2003). The band pass filter.
*International Economic Review*, 44(2), 435–465.

## D

### Diebolt & Doliger (2008) {#diebolt-doliger-2008}

Diebolt, C., & Doliger, C. (2008). New international evidence on the
cyclical behaviour of output: Kondratieff and beyond. *Applied Economics*,
40(13), 1675–1684.

## G

### Goldstein (1988) {#goldstein-1988}

Goldstein, J. S. (1988). *Long cycles: prosperity and war in the modern age*.
Yale University Press.

### Gordon (2016) {#gordon-2016}

Gordon, R. J. (2016). *The rise and fall of American growth: the U.S. standard
of living since the Civil War*. Princeton University Press.

### Grinsted, Moore & Jevrejeva (2004) {#grinsted-moore-jevrejeva-2004}

Grinsted, A., Moore, J. C., & Jevrejeva, S. (2004). Application of the cross

*Nonlinear Processes in Geophysics*, 11, 561–566.

## H

### Hamilton (1989) {#hamilton-1989}

Hamilton, J. D. (1989). A new approach to the economic analysis of
nonstationary time series and the business cycle. *Econometrica*, 57(2),
357–384.

### Hamilton (2018) {#hamilton-2018}

Hamilton, J. D. (2018). Why you should never use the Hodrick-Prescott
filter. *Review of Economics and Statistics*, 100(5), 831–843.

### Harding & Pagan (2002) {#harding-pagan-2002}

Harding, D., & Pagan, A. (2002). Dissecting the cycle: a methodological

## J

### Jordà, Schularick & Taylor (2017) {#jorda-schularick-taylor-2017}

Jordà, Ò., Schularick, M., & Taylor, A. M. (2017). Macrofinancial history
and the new business cycle facts. *NBER Macroeconomics Annual*, 31(1), 213–263.

### Juglar (1862) {#juglar-1862}

Juglar, C. (1862). *Des crises commerciales et de leur retour périodique en
France, en Angleterre et aux États-Unis*. Guillaumin.

## K

### Killick, Fearnhead & Eckley (2012) {#killick-fearnhead-eckley-2012}

Killick, R., Fearnhead, P., & Eckley, I. A. (2012). Optimal detection of

Statistical Association*, 107(500), 1590–1598.

### Kitchin (1923) {#kitchin-1923}

Kitchin, J. (1923). Cycles and trends in economic factors. *Review of

Kondratieff, N. D. (1925). The major economic cycles. *Voprosy Konjunktury*,

### Korotayev & Tsirel (2010) {#korotayev-tsirel-2010}

Korotayev, A. V., & Tsirel, S. V. (2010). A spectral analysis of world GDP
dynamics: Kondratieff waves, Kuznets swings, Juglar and Kitchin cycles in

### Kose, Otrok & Whiteman (2003) {#kose-otrok-whiteman-2003}

Kose, M. A., Otrok, C., & Whiteman, C. H. (2003). International business
